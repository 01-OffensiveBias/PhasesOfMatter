Phase Changes
Freezing / Melting
Melting is the process of a change in phase from a solid to a liquid. This occurs when there is enough energy absorbed by the solid that its molecular bonds loosen and its rigid structure broken apart, allowing the molecules to flow over each other to fill its container. Freezing is the opposite, where enough heat is taken away from a liquid that the molecular bonds become strong enough to form rigid structures.



Vaporization/Condensation
Vaporization is the process of a change in phase from a liquid to a gas. This occurs when there is enough energy absorbed by the liquid that its molecular bonds are broken almost completely which allows the molecules to float freely and the gas to expand and fill its container. The opposite of this process is Condensation where a gas loses enough molecular energy to change phase into a liquid.



Deposition/Sublimation
Deposition is the process of a change in phase from a gas directly to a solid. This process skips liquid form and goes straight to gas. Deposition can happen with certain materials at certain pressures and temperatures. Sublimation is the opposite process where a solid changes phase directly to a gas.



Ionization/Recombination
Ioniziation is the process of a change in phase from a gas to a plasma. This process happens with large amounts of molecular energy breaking apart the electrons from the atoms. A plasma is essentially a bunch of nuclei floating in a soup of electrons.
